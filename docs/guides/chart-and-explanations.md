# Price Chart & "What am I looking at?"

The main screen has two halves: the **price chart** (left) and the **explanation
panel** (right). The explanation panel is the heart of the tool's *teaching* idea —
it reads the indicators for you and says what to do, using the stock's live numbers.

## The candlestick chart
Each candle is one **day** (or one **week** in the Weekly view):
- **Green** candle = price closed **up** that period; **red** = closed **down**.
- The thick body is open→close; the thin wicks are the high and low.
- Drag to pan, scroll to zoom. The chart auto-fits when you switch symbols.

**Overlays on the chart:**
- **Orange / purple lines** = the 20- and 50-period EMAs (trend).
- **Dashed blue lines** = Bollinger Bands (volatility envelope around a 20-period mean).
- **Bars at the bottom** = volume (green on up days, red on down days).

**Daily ↔ Weekly toggle** (top-right of the Analyze screen): Daily is for timing an
entry; Weekly shows the bigger trend. As a swing/positional trader (2wk–6mo), check the
**Weekly** trend first, then use **Daily** to time the entry.

## The explanation panel — how to read it
For each indicator you'll see a coloured **stance** tag and four lines:

| Line | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Stance** (green/red/grey) | Quick verdict: **bullish**, **bearish**, or **neutral**. |
| *summary* (grey) | What this indicator measures, in one line. |
| **What it's saying now** | The meaning of the **current** value for *this* stock. |
| **What to do** | The action a trader typically takes in this situation. |
| ⚠ *caveat* | When the signal is unreliable / what would invalidate it. |

The indicators shown today:
- **EMA Trend (20 vs 50):** is the stock in an up- or down-trend?
- **RSI (14):** is momentum overstretched (overbought >70 / oversold <30)?
- **MACD (12,26,9):** is momentum building or fading, and which way?

## How to actually use it (short-term trader workflow)
1. **Check the trend first (EMA).** Prefer trades in the trend's direction.
2. **Time it with momentum (RSI/MACD).** In an uptrend, a dip to a neutral/oversold
   RSI is a better entry than buying when it's already overbought.
3. **Look for agreement.** When EMA, RSI, and MACD all lean the same way, the signal
   is stronger. When they disagree, that's a "wait" — note *why* from each caveat.
4. **Respect the caveats.** They tell you when *not* to trust a reading (e.g. choppy
   market, low volume). Avoiding bad trades matters as much as catching good ones.

## Good to know
- These are **decision aids, not advice** — you make the call.
- Richer signals (Bollinger, volume, patterns), multi-timeframe, fundamentals, and the
  correlation/FII-DII engine arrive in later phases (see `PLAN.md` §7).
