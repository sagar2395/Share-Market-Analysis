# Patterns & Key Levels

On the **Analyze** screen, below the confluence badge, the **Patterns & key levels** card
reads the recent candles for you and marks the nearest **support** and **resistance** —
the price levels where a swing trade's risk and reward are decided.

## Support & resistance
- **Support** = the nearest price *below* where buyers have stepped in before (a floor).
- **Resistance** = the nearest price *above* where sellers have appeared before (a ceiling).
- The card tells you how far price is from each. Useful because good swing entries are
  **near support** (low risk, stop just below) and targets often sit **near resistance**.

## Candlestick patterns
When the latest bar forms a recognised pattern, it's named and explained:
| Pattern | Direction | What it hints |
|---|---|---|
| **Hammer** | Bullish | Sellers pushed down, buyers reclaimed — possible bounce (esp. at support). |
| **Bullish engulfing** | Bullish | A strong green bar swallows the prior red one — buyers took control. |
| **Shooting star** | Bearish | Buyers pushed up, sellers slammed it back — rally may stall. |
| **Bearish engulfing** | Bearish | A strong red bar swallows the prior green one — protect profits. |
| **Doji** | Neutral | Indecision — wait for the next candle to confirm. |

## How to use it (with everything else)
A pattern is a **hint, not a trigger**. It's strongest when it lines up with the rest:
1. **Confluence badge** says the trend supports a long.
2. Price is **near support**.
3. A **bullish pattern** prints there.
4. Volume confirms.

That stack is a high-quality swing entry — and the stop goes just below the support/pattern
low. Bearish patterns near resistance are your cue to trim or tighten stops (you trade
long-only, so they mean "protect", not "short").

## Notes
- Patterns are detected on the **current timeframe** (Daily or Weekly toggle).
- Bullish/bearish labels are colour-coded (green/red) in the card.
