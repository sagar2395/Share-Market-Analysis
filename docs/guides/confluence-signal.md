# Weekly + Daily Confluence (the signal badge)

At the top of the **Analyze** screen you'll see one **badge** that sums up the setup by
combining two timeframes — exactly how a swing/positional trader should look at a chart:

- **Weekly trend** = the *tide* (the big direction over your 2wk–6mo horizon).
- **Daily trend** = the *timing* (where to get in).

When they agree, odds improve. When they fight, the badge tells you to wait.

## The badges
| Badge | What it means | Typical action |
|---|---|---|
| **Trend aligned ↑** | Weekly and daily both up — cleanest long backdrop. | **Buy** strength or the next small dip; trail a stop under the 20-EMA. |
| **Buy-the-dip zone** | Weekly up, daily pulled back into a buyable area. | **Buy** on a reversal signal; stop below the dip. The sweet spot for swings. |
| **Extended — wait** | Uptrend but daily is overbought / at the upper band. | **Watch** — chasing here is poor risk-reward; wait for a pullback. |
| **Counter-trend bounce — caution** | Daily bouncing but weekly still down. | **Watch** — bounces in downtrends often fail; wait for the weekly to turn. |
| **Downtrend — avoid** | Weekly down. | **Avoid** — you're long-only, so stand aside until the trend repairs. |
| **Basing / oversold** | No weekly trend, daily oversold. | **Watch** for a bounce; not a trend trade yet. |
| **Range / mixed — wait** | Timeframes disagree / no trend. | **Hold** — patience until they line up. |

## The score (0–100)
A confidence/quality number for the *swing setup*, built from trend agreement, momentum
(RSI/MACD), and whether price is overextended. Higher = cleaner setup. It's a **ranking
aid**, not a probability — use it to compare candidates, not as a guarantee.

## How to use it
1. Read the **badge** first — it's the headline.
2. Check the **reasons** listed below it (weekly/daily trend, RSI, MACD) so you
   understand *why* — this is the teaching layer; over time you'll read charts the same way.
3. Respect the **action**: "avoid" and "wait" save you from low-quality trades, which
   matters as much as finding good ones.
4. Always set your **stop** before entering (the badge reminds you).
